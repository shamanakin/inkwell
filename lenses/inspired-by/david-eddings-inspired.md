## Style Rules

**Sentence Structure**:
Primarily clear and accessible. Mix of medium-length sentences (15-25 words) with shorter ones for emphasis. Longer sentences when building epic scope. Fragments rare unless for specific effect.

**Jargon Policy**:
Explain terms naturally through context. Use accessible language. Technical terms when they add precision, but always with context.

**Metaphor & Imagery**:
Frequent and accessible. Concrete, vivid imagery. Help readers see and feel. Metaphors should clarify, not obscure.

**Examples & Stories**:
Heavy use of concrete examples. Stories that drive narrative. Make abstract concepts tangible through specific instances.

**Paragraph Length**:
Medium to short. Break for clarity, for emphasis, for pacing. Longer paragraphs when building epic scope or developing complex ideas.

**Formatting**:
Clear structure with headers when helpful. Flowing prose preferred. Use formatting to guide through epic scope.

---

## Rhythm Constraints

**Punctuation Distribution:**
- Em dashes: Maximum 1-2 per entire chapter/section (HARD CONSTRAINT)
- Prefer periods and commas. Use punctuation for clarity.
- Vary sentence length within paragraphs.

**Structural Variance:**
- Rotate transition patterns. Avoid formulaic scaffolding.
- Mix paragraph lengths. Don't cluster similar structures.
- Avoid triadic structures and mirrored clauses.

**Semantic Density:**
- One clear point per paragraph.
- Advance narrative clearly, don't just restate.

---

## Process Preferences

**Before Drafting**:
Plan clear structure. Know where story progresses. Plan character development and epic scope.

**During Drafting**:
Check that structure is clear. Verify accessibility. Ensure epic scope doesn't confuse.

**Handling Ambiguity**:
Choose clarity. When in doubt, make it accessible. Note trade-offs between epic scope and clarity.

- Clear and accessible
- Epic scope with clarity
- Character-driven narrative
- Clear progression

**Revision Triggers**:
- Complexity that confuses
- Losing clarity for scope
- Structure that doesn't serve
- Flat, no progression

**Polish Indicators**:
- Epic scope is accessible
- Structure guides clearly
- Character drives narrative
- Progression is clear

## Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | Symptom | Self-Check | Correction |
|--------------|---------|------------|------------|
| Overly complex | Confusing, loses clarity | Can a thoughtful reader follow this? | Simplify, add clarity |
| Losing scope | Too focused, no epic feel | Is the scope epic enough? | Add scope, maintain clarity |
| Losing structure | Unclear progression | Is the structure clear? | Add structure, clarify progression |
| Flat | No character, no drive | Does character drive narrative? | Add character development, drive |
| Em dash overuse | More than 1-2 per chapter | Count em dashes | Replace with periods/commas |
